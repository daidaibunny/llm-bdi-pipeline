\section{Experimental Evaluation}
\label{sec:evaluation}

\subsection{Benchmarks and Protocol}

\paragraph{Corpus and repetitions.}
The corpus comprises eight classical and four bounded-integer MOOSE domains with
official splits \cite{Chen2026Moose,DillonZChenMooseDataset}, plus four
predeclared serialized-width families \cite{LipovetzkyGeffner2012Width}:
Blocksworld Clear and On
\cite{Frances2021LearningGeneralPolicies,BonetLearnerPoliciesRepository},
Tower \cite{Bacchus2001AIPS00,PotasscoPDDLInstances}, and Depots
\cite{Bonet2018Features,RLeapD2LRepository}. MOOSE readable policies provide
$\mathcal E_{\mathrm{raw}}$. Each of five fixed seeds follows MOOSE by using all
training instances and three goal orders to compile one core per domain (80 total).

\paragraph{Temporal benchmark construction.}
For each held-out problem, we replace the original goal with one of five
predeclared predicate or bounded-integer profiles: $F(A\land B)$, $F(A\land\neg B)$,
$F(A\land X(F(B)))$,
$F(A\land X(F(B\land X(F(C)))))$, and strong $A\,U\,B$.
The lifted hidden formula is rendered deterministically as controlled English.
GPT-5.5 \cite{OpenAI2026GPT55SystemCard} translates 475 deduplicated inputs,
expanding to 1,228 witness-backed bound queries.

\paragraph{Controlled inputs and validation.}
Atomic variants share each seed's evidence; temporal variants share the core,
binding, and DFA. Atomic success requires Jason completion and original-goal
VAL \cite{Bordini2007Jason,Howey2004VAL}; translation requires exact predicted--gold
DFA language equivalence; temporal success requires compilation, Jason,
neutral-goal VAL, and both DFA oracles. Failures remain in the denominator.
Comparisons pair held-out identifiers and average seeded atomic outcomes before
inference. The Technical Supplement, Secs.~4--5 gives construction, limits,
costs, and inference rules.

\subsection{Paired Component Ablations}
\label{sec:paired-ablation-results}

\paragraph{Atomic compilation.}
Evidence Only validates provider macros; Direct Producers adds action-only
producers; Maximum Feasible adds recursive preparation and resource discharge
and retains a maximum-cardinality feasible subset. Full GP2PL applies
lexicographic Clingo selection to those candidates, isolating target expansion,
recursion, and compact selection.

Direct Producers, Maximum Feasible, and Full GP2PL cover all 73 producible

Table~\ref{tab:atomic-comparison}, entirely on the four added families; every
nonzero paired difference favors it. At equal coverage, lexicographic selection

\paragraph{Temporal composition.}
Unprotected Serialization omits completion-effect analysis. Module-Return
Monitor retains certified balanced repair but checks the DFA only after an
atomic module finishes all primitive actions; Certified Balanced checks after
each action. All share the automaton, library, and monitor implementation.

Certified Balanced gains 9.36 points over Unprotected Serialization
(Table~\ref{tab:temporal-comparison}); 113 of 115 gains are in bounded-integer
domains and two in Transport. We select its bounded local structure, not its
runtime; helper plans are the cost. Lower Module-Return coverage supports
primitive-step observation.

\paragraph{End-to-end temporal validation.}
\label{sec:end-to-end-results}
The paired temporal study fixes one seed-0 core per domain, isolating controller
variants. Its five structures instantiate $\Phi_{\mathrm{bench}}$, not all of
$\Phi_{\mathrm{syn}}$; execution includes queries admitted to
$\Phi_{\mathrm{cert}}(D,\mathcal M_D)$. All
\TranslationEquivalentCount{} translations are DFA-language equivalent; all
\TEGProblemCount{} admitted queries pass Jason, neutral-goal VAL, and both DFA
oracles. Median runtime is \TEGMedianRuntimeSeconds{} seconds (IQR 4.28--8.49),
with \TEGMedianActionCount{} actions (IQR 1--2) per median trace.
Across 740 same-seed added-family cases, GP2PL raises VAL-valid
coverage from 15.8\% to 97.3\%; original-scope MOOSE results are separate
\cite{Chen2025MooseExtended}.
