\subsection{Questions and Comparisons}

\paragraph{Atomic compilation.}
\emph{Evidence Only} validates provider macros against the PDDL schemas and
retains them without producible-target expansion. \emph{Direct Producers} adds
action-only producers for the producible target universe; \emph{Maximum
Feasible} retains a maximum-cardinality jointly feasible subset after adding
compatible recursive precondition-repair and target-preserving
resource-discharge candidates; and \emph{Full GP2PL} applies the lexicographic
Clingo objective to the same generated certified set. Their paired
comparison measures target coverage, internal-call closure, held-out execution,
and compact selection of the atomic core.

\paragraph{Temporal composition.}
\emph{Unprotected Serialization} uses the same automaton, library, and
primitive-step monitor without completion-effect analysis. \emph{Certified
Flat} adds threat-induced precedence ordering and preservation portfolios;
\emph{Certified Balanced} changes only
flat control to the balanced binary transition-repair tree. A
\emph{Module-Return Monitor} observes only on module return,
testing the primitive-step observation boundary.

\paragraph{End-to-end references.}
End-to-end evaluation measures valid traces and amortized compilation. Raw
MOOSE, LAMA, ENHSP, and FOND4LTLf with LAMA
\cite{Chen2026Moose,RichterWestphal2010LAMA,
Scala2020NumericSubgoaling,DeGiacomoFuggitti2021FOND4LTLf} are task-level
references, not compiler ablations. The MOOSE comparison is partitioned by
provenance before scoring. For all twelve domains in its original benchmark, we
report the five-model mean coverage from Table~4 of the extended paper
\cite{Chen2025MooseExtended}. For all four GP2PL-added domains, we measure Raw
MOOSE locally over seeds 0--4. We never select a source separately for an
individual domain after observing its outcome.

\subsection{Benchmarks and Protocol}

\paragraph{Corpus and repetitions.}
The corpus comprises eight classical and four bounded-integer MOOSE domains with
official splits \cite{Chen2026Moose,DillonZChenMooseDataset}, plus four
serialized-width families \cite{LipovetzkyGeffner2012Width}. Blocksworld Clear
and On use published no-constants splits
\cite{Frances2021LearningGeneralPolicies,
BonetLearnerPoliciesRepository}; Tower uses the first quarter of a fixed IPC
corpus \cite{Bacchus2001AIPS00,PotasscoPDDLInstances}, and Depots the first half
of the 22-instance D2L corpus
\cite{Bonet2018Features,RLeapD2LRepository}. Groups are used only for reporting.
Following MOOSE \cite{Chen2026Moose}, the atomic study uses all training
instances, three goal orders, 12 hours, and seeds 0--4. Each seed runs with one
internal worker and is analyzed independently. Our 16~GiB cap differs from
MOOSE's 32~GB; Jason and VAL receive 30 minutes per test.
Source revisions and splits appear in the Technical Supplement, Sec.~4.3;
parameters and reporting rules appear in Secs.~5.2--5.3.

\paragraph{Controlled inputs and references.}
Atomic variants share each seed's evidence; temporal variants share the atomic
core, binding, and DFA, with pairing established by input fingerprints. MOOSE reuses
its learned model; LAMA
\cite{RichterWestphal2010LAMA} and ENHSP MRP+HJ
\cite{Scala2020NumericSubgoaling} are the classical and numeric references used
by MOOSE \cite{Chen2026Moose}. FOND4LTLf v0.0.4
\cite{DeGiacomoFuggitti2021FOND4LTLf} with LAMA covers its accepted grounded
Boolean subset. References receive 30 minutes and 8~GB per instance.
The published MOOSE row is coverage-only: it is labelled Reported rather than
locally reproduced, and its time and memory are not compared across hardware.
The local Raw MOOSE extension row is labelled Measured and reports mean
$\pm$ sample standard deviation over five independently trained models. The
original and added scopes contain 1,080 and 148 cases per seed, respectively.

\paragraph{Validation and measures.}
A temporal run succeeds only if Jason completes \cite{Bordini2007Jason}, VAL
accepts the full primitive trace against a neutral PDDL goal
\cite{Howey2004VAL}, and independent replay is accepted by both DFAs. Failed
prefixes are diagnostic; atomic validation uses the original PDDL goal. We
report internal-call closure, held-out success, size, runtime, actions, and trigger fan-out;
plan length uses jointly solved instances. The challenge suite tests rejection
and symbol invariance. Figure~\ref{fig:evaluation-summary} follows MOOSE's
absolute-time presentation \cite{Chen2026Moose}: a case enters a runtime curve
only after its committed trace passes VAL, and all failures remain in the
denominator. Tables~\ref{tab:five-seed-atomic} and
\ref{tab:profile-results} report the compact atomic and temporal-profile
outcomes. The Technical Supplement reports every domain--seed cell and the
fixed seed-0 atomic-core inputs used by the temporal study. The result
subsections interpret these preregistered views without selecting domains after
execution.

\subsection{Five-Seed Atomic Results}
\label{sec:atomic-results}

The five independently compiled atomic cores solve, for seeds 0--4,
\AtomicSeedZeroSuccessCount{}, \AtomicSeedOneSuccessCount{},
\AtomicSeedTwoSuccessCount{}, \AtomicSeedThreeSuccessCount{}, and
\AtomicSeedFourSuccessCount{} of \AtomicFiveSeedCaseCount{} held-out cases.
Their mean is \AtomicFiveSeedMeanSuccessPercent{}\% $\pm$
\AtomicFiveSeedSampleSDPercent{} percentage points. All
\AtomicFiveSeedPooledSuccessCount{} successful traces pass original-goal VAL,
with no timeout or nonzero exit. Fourteen domains are complete,
\AtomicFiveSeedAllSuccessCount{} distinct cases succeed for every seed,
\AtomicFiveSeedSensitiveCount{} are seed-sensitive, and only
\AtomicFiveSeedPersistentFailureCount{} fail for all five. The pooled
\AtomicFiveSeedPooledSuccessCount{}/\AtomicFiveSeedPooledEvaluationCount{}
count comprises repeated outcomes, not independent cases. Figure~
\ref{fig:evaluation-summary} includes every domain and the three predeclared
groups, never a post-hoc domain subset.

Variation is localized: Logistics ranges from 54/90 to 90/90 because seeded
goal order changes available cross-mode provider macros, while action-schema-derived
candidate generation cannot admit the cyclic \texttt{at}/\texttt{in} dependency
without a decreasing certificate. Depots obtains 63.64\% $\pm$ 11.13 points; \texttt{p12} and
\texttt{p20} require a nested support-resource choice beyond the already-bound
capacity certificate. The Logistics and Depots failures therefore mark the
boundary of the candidate-construction grammar; every reported success remains a
VAL-valid trace. No domain-specific rule was introduced after observing
held-out outcomes.
Table~\ref{tab:five-seed-atomic} gives the non-pooled five-seed summary; the
Technical Supplement, Sec.~5.3 gives every domain--seed count, provenance, and
failure diagnostic.

\subsection{End-to-End Temporal Results}
\label{sec:end-to-end-results}

The temporal protocol exposes the public PDDL vocabulary, typed parameters, and
binding constraints; it admits $F$, $X$, strong $U$, conjunction, and literal
negation. The controlled source utterances specify the evaluation distribution,
not a required user grammar. GPT-5.5 \cite{OpenAI2026GPT55SystemCard}
translates \TranslationInputCount{} deduplicated specifications. Every accepted
translation satisfies the typed structural validation conditions and exact DFA-language
equivalence, yielding \TEGProblemCount{} bound queries. The serialized schema
and validation protocol appear in the Technical Supplement, Sec.~4.2.

Table~\ref{tab:profile-results} separates five formula profiles. Every query
completes in Jason; all \TEGVALSuccessCount{} nonempty traces pass VAL, and both
DFAs accept all \TEGGoldDFAAcceptCount{} traces. Runtime is
\TEGMedianRuntimeSeconds{} seconds median (IQR 4.28--8.49), and action count is
\TEGMedianActionCount{} median (IQR 1--2). All 13 rejection and
symbol-invariance challenges pass.
