\section{Experimental Evaluation}
\label{sec:evaluation}

\subsection{Benchmarks and Protocol}

\paragraph{Corpus and repetitions.}
The corpus contains eight classical and four bounded-integer MOOSE domains with
official splits \cite{Chen2026Moose,DillonZChenMooseDataset} and four
predeclared serialized-width families \cite{LipovetzkyGeffner2012Width}:
Blocksworld Clear and On
\cite{Frances2021LearningGeneralPolicies,BonetLearnerPoliciesRepository},
Tower \cite{Bacchus2001AIPS00,PotasscoPDDLInstances}, and Depots
\cite{Bonet2018Features,RLeapD2LRepository}. MOOSE readable policies provide
$\mathcal E_{\mathrm{raw}}$. Following MOOSE, each of five fixed seeds determines
three goal orders over every training instance, producing one core per domain (80
total).

\paragraph{Temporal benchmark construction.}
For each held-out problem, benchmark construction discards the original goal and uses
predicate events and bounded-integer changes to instantiate five predeclared
profiles: $F(A\land B)$, $F(A\land\neg B)$,
$F(A\land X(F(B)))$,
$F(A\land X(F(B\land X(F(C)))))$, and strong $A\,U\,B$.
Each hidden formula is lifted and rendered deterministically as
controlled English. GPT-5.5 \cite{OpenAI2026GPT55SystemCard} translates 475
deduplicated inputs, which expand to 1,228 witness-backed bound queries.
The five profiles contain only proper TEGs; the atomic-core study evaluates
achievement conditions.

\paragraph{Controlled inputs and validation.}
Atomic variants share each seed's evidence; temporal variants share the core,
binding, and DFA. Atomic success requires Jason completion and original-goal
VAL \cite{Bordini2007Jason,Howey2004VAL}; translation requires exact predicted--gold
DFA language equivalence; temporal success requires compilation, Jason,
neutral-goal VAL, and acceptance by both DFAs. Every failure remains in the
denominator. Comparisons are paired by held-out identifier, averaging seeded
atomic outcomes within identifiers before inference. Construction details,
limits, costs, and inference rules appear in the Technical Supplement,
Secs.~4--5.

\subsection{Paired Component Ablations}
\label{sec:paired-ablation-results}

\paragraph{Atomic compilation.}
Evidence Only validates provider macros; Direct Producers adds action-only
producers for the producible target universe; Maximum Feasible adds certified
recursive preparation and target-preserving resource discharge and retains a
maximum-cardinality feasible subset. Full GP2PL applies lexicographic Clingo
selection to the same candidates. The rows isolate target expansion, recursive
generation, and compact selection.

Sec.~5.3.

\paragraph{Temporal composition.}
Unprotected Serialization omits completion-effect analysis. Module-Return
Monitor retains ordering, preservation portfolios, and balanced repair but
checks the DFA only after an atomic module finishes all primitive actions;
Certified Balanced checks after every primitive action. All share the
automaton, library, and monitor.

\subsection{End-to-End Temporal Validation}
\label{sec:end-to-end-results}
All \TranslationEquivalentCount{}/\TranslationInputCount{} translated
specifications are DFA-language equivalent to their gold formulas. Across five
independently compiled atomic-core sets, Certified Balanced validates all
\TemporalCrossSeedValidCount{} admitted executions
(\TemporalCrossSeedQueryCount{} queries under each seed), and no admitted query
changes outcome across seeds. Primitive-action counts are invariant across seeds, and mean per-seed
PAR-2 is \TemporalCrossSeedMeanParTwoSeconds{} $\pm$
\TemporalCrossSeedParTwoSDSeconds{} seconds. Breakdowns by temporal profile,
runtime, and trace length appear in the Technical Supplement, Sec.~5.3.

\paragraph{External task references.}
On the same achievement corpus, LAMA validates
\ExternalLAMAValidCount{}/\ExternalLAMACaseCount{} classical instances and
ENHSP MRP+HJ validates
\ExternalMRPHJValidCount{}/\ExternalMRPHJCaseCount{} bounded-integer instances
\cite{RichterWestphal2010LAMA,Scala2020NumericSubgoaling}; Full GP2PL averages
\AtomicFiveSeedMeanSuccessPercent{}\% across all 1,228 held-out cases. Among the
Boolean temporal queries admitted by FOND4LTLf, GP2PL validates all
\ExternalDirectSupportedCount{}, whereas FOND4LTLf with LAMA validates
\ExternalDirectValidCount{}/\ExternalDirectSupportedCount{} (PAR-2
\ExternalDirectParTwoSeconds{} seconds) \cite{DeGiacomoFuggitti2021FOND4LTLf}.
The other \ExternalDirectUnsupportedCount{} queries are outside FOND4LTLf's declared
scope. TIDE with LAMA validates
\ExternalTIDEValidCount{}/\ExternalTIDESupportedCount{} Boolean queries (PAR-2
\ExternalTIDEParTwoSeconds{} seconds); the \ExternalTIDEUnsupportedCount{}
numeric queries are outside TIDE's scope \cite{Suprun2026TIDE}. Complete
scope-separated results and protocols appear in the Technical Supplement,
Sec.~5.3.
