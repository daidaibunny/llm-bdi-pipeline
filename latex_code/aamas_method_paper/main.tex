\begin{abstract}
Generalized-planning policies transfer across related instances, but are not
automatically executable plan libraries for Belief--Desire--Intention (BDI)
agents. Direct transcription does not establish range restriction, internal-call
closure, recursive progress, resource restoration, or compatibility among
temporal achievements. We formulate \methodname{} (Generalized Planning to Plan
Libraries), a certificate-carrying compilation framework for this representation
gap. At the domain level, it combines singleton-goal policy evidence with PDDL
action schemas and selects a compact, closed set of lifted BDI modules under
binding, execution, completion, progress, and resource-restoration obligations.
At the query level, it derives controllers from deterministic automata constructed
by \texttt{ltlf2dfa} and MONA, admitting only transition strategies that preserve
signed guard obligations.
Theorems establish local module soundness, candidate-space optimality,
transition-composition soundness, and trace-faithful monitoring for the declared
fragment; obligations lacking the required certificates are rejected. We
instantiate the framework with MOOSE evidence, Clingo selection, and an
\asl{}/Jason realization. Across
\BenchmarkDomainCount{} domains, all \TranslationEquivalentCount{}
model-produced specifications are language-equivalent to their gold formulas,
and all \TEGProblemCount{} bound queries produce traces accepted by Jason, VAL,
and both finite-trace automata.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Classical PDDL planning returns action sequences for individual grounded
problems \cite{McDermott1998PDDL}; generalized planning instead seeks reusable
behavior across a family
\cite{Srivastava2011DirectedSearch,Chen2026Moose}. A
Belief--Desire--Intention (BDI) interpreter executes a plan library connecting
events and belief contexts to actions and subgoals
\cite{Rao1996AgentSpeak,MeneguzziDeSilva2015PlanningBDI}. Conversion must
therefore establish variable binding, internal-call closure, well-founded
recursion, and evidence coverage.

For example, singleton-goal evidence for \texttt{on(X,Y)} may authorize
\texttt{stack(X,Y)} under \texttt{holding(X)} and \texttt{clear(Y)}
\cite{Chen2026Moose}. A reusable module must also clear an obstructed
\texttt{Y}, although \texttt{clear} may be absent when it was never a training
goal. PDDL schemas must then supply the module and certify recursive progress.

Temporally extended goals pose a separate problem
\cite{DeGiacomoVardi2013,DeGiacomoRubin2018AutomataFOND}. The formula
$F(\mathit{on}(X,Y)\land X(F(\mathit{clear}(Z))))$ imposes query-specific
order over reusable modules. Conjunctive automaton-guard literals must hold in
one observed state, so later modules cannot undo earlier achievements, a form
of BDI goal interference \cite{Thangarajah2003GoalInterference}. Temporal
control must therefore be composed over the fixed domain library.

Figure~\ref{fig:architecture} presents \methodname{}. Domain compilation joins
singleton-goal evidence with PDDL schemas once; query compilation turns
supported deterministic finite automaton (DFA) transitions into controllers.
We instantiate these interfaces with MOOSE evidence \cite{Chen2026Moose} and an
\asl{}/Jason plan library \cite{Rao1996AgentSpeak,Bordini2007Jason}.
Certificates use rule structure and symbolic PDDL effects, never names.

GP2PL contributes (i) a candidate language with minimum-cost evidence-to-library
selection; (ii) symbol-invariant certificates for binding, execution,
achievement, recursion, closure, and resource restoration; and (iii)
preservation-safe \ltlf-to-DFA guard composition under primitive-step monitoring.
Uncertified modules and transitions are rejected. Across
\BenchmarkDomainCount{} domains, all \TEGProblemCount{} evaluated queries
execute in Jason, pass VAL, and are accepted by both gold and predicted DFAs.
This is evaluated-input coverage, not completeness for arbitrary
PDDL--\ltlf{} products; complete proofs and the reproducibility protocol appear
in the Technical Supplement, Secs.~2 and~5.

\section{Conclusion}

GP2PL compiles generalized-planning evidence into certified BDI plan libraries
and composes finite-trace DFA progress over them. Its \asl{}/Jason realization
passes Jason, neutral-goal VAL, and both DFA checks on all \TEGProblemCount{}
queries in \BenchmarkDomainCount{} domains. Guarantees remain conditional on the
candidate language and declared \ltlf{} fragment; uncertified strategies are
rejected.
