\begin{abstract}
Generalized-planning policies are not directly executable as
Belief--Desire--Intention (BDI) plan libraries: transcription alone does not
establish variable binding, internal-call closure, recursive progress, resource
restoration, or temporal compatibility. We introduce
\methodname{} (Generalized Planning to Plan Libraries), a certificate-carrying
compiler that closes this representation gap. Domain compilation combines
singleton-goal policy evidence with PDDL action schemas into a certified atomic
module core; query compilation composes preservation-safe plans from
deterministic-automaton guards without relearning that core. For the declared
candidate language and temporal fragment, we prove local module soundness,
candidate-space optimality, transition-composition soundness, and trace-faithful
primitive-step monitoring; obligations without the required certificates are
rejected. The evaluated instantiation combines MOOSE, Clingo,
\texttt{ltlf2dfa}/MONA, and \asl{}/Jason. Across \BenchmarkDomainCount{}
domains, five independently seeded atomic cores obtain
\AtomicFiveSeedMeanSuccessPercent{}\% mean held-out Jason-plus-VAL coverage
(\AtomicFiveSeedSampleSDPercent{} percentage-point sample standard deviation).
All \TranslationEquivalentCount{} translated specifications are
language-equivalent to their gold formulas, and all \TEGProblemCount{} bound
queries produce traces accepted by Jason, VAL, and both finite-trace automata.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Classical PDDL planning returns action sequences for individual grounded
problems \cite{McDermott1998PDDL}; generalized planning instead seeks reusable
behavior across a family
\cite{Srivastava2011DirectedSearch,Chen2026Moose}. A
Belief--Desire--Intention (BDI) interpreter executes a plan library connecting
events and belief contexts to actions and subgoals
\cite{Rao1996AgentSpeak,MeneguzziDeSilva2015PlanningBDI}. Conversion must
therefore establish variable binding, internal-call closure, well-founded
recursion, and evidence coverage.

Singleton-goal evidence for \texttt{on(X,Y)} may authorize
\texttt{stack(X,Y)} under \texttt{holding(X)} and \texttt{clear(Y)} while
omitting \texttt{clear} as a target \cite{Chen2026Moose}. GP2PL includes every
positively producible predicate in a domain-wide target universe, generates an
internal \texttt{clear} module from its producer schemas, and accepts recursive
use only with a decreasing relational ranking.

Temporally extended goals pose a separate problem
\cite{DeGiacomoVardi2013,DeGiacomoRubin2018AutomataFOND}. The formula
$F(\mathit{on}(X,Y)\land X(F(\mathit{clear}(Z))))$ imposes query-specific
order over reusable modules. Conjunctive automaton-guard literals must hold in
one observed state, so later modules cannot undo earlier achievements, a form
of BDI goal interference \cite{Thangarajah2003GoalInterference}. Temporal
control must therefore be composed over the fixed domain library.

Figure~\ref{fig:architecture} separates the one-time atomic module core
$\mathcal M_D$ from successive query-local additions. After $k$ appends, a new
bound query $q$ updates the unique library by
$\mathcal L_D^{[k+1]}=\mathcal L_D^{[k]}\cup\mathcal Q_q$. Both stages are
symbol-invariant under consistent renaming: domain certificates depend on
action-schema structure and symbolic effects, while query certificates depend
on signed DFA guards and conditional module summaries.

Figure~\ref{fig:policy-lifting-example} follows one atomic target through
evidence normalization, producible-target expansion, schema candidate
generation, certificate checking, feasible-library selection, and construction
of the certified atomic module core.

GP2PL contributes (i) a finite candidate language with lexicographically
optimal evidence-to-core selection inside that language; (ii) symbol-invariant
certificates for binding, execution,
achievement, recursion, internal-call closure, and resource restoration; and (iii)
preservation-safe \ltlf-to-DFA guard composition under primitive-step monitoring.
Uncertified modules and transitions are rejected. Across
\BenchmarkDomainCount{} domains, independent atomic cores average
\AtomicFiveSeedMeanSuccessPercent{}\% held-out Jason-plus-VAL coverage over
five MOOSE goal-order seeds, while all \TEGProblemCount{} predeclared
temporal queries pass Jason, VAL, and both DFAs. These are evaluated-input
coverage results, not completeness for arbitrary PDDL--\ltlf{} products;
complete proofs and the reproducibility protocol appear in the Technical
Supplement, Secs.~2 and~5.

\section{Conclusion}

GP2PL compiles generalized-planning evidence into a certified atomic module core
and appends finite-trace query plans to the unique maintained BDI domain library.
Five-seed atomic coverage averages
\AtomicFiveSeedMeanSuccessPercent{}\%; the predeclared temporal study solves
\TEGProblemCount{}/\TEGProblemCount{}. Guarantees remain conditional on the
certified candidate language and declared \ltlf{} fragment.
